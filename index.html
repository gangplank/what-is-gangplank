Gangplank is building the new economy.

In the years leading up to the Great Recession, Arizona’s employment growth rate was around 4% or 5% each year, second highest in the nation. Population explosion expanded the construction and service industries, which dominated employment growth in Arizona. Despite being top in job growth, the number of Arizonans enrolling in ACCHHS kept rising. Currently, the number of Arizonans at or below the poverty level is 50% higher than the national average.

These statistics emphasize job growth alone will not sustain an economy. Quality jobs in opportunity industries raise the standard of living. While effective economic development strategy has us continue to pursue those types of companies, forecasting shows manufacturing is shrinking and the days of large companies moving to Arizona for its low cost, high quality value proposition are numbered. The desire for a long-term, healthy economy demands a focus on innovation and allowing people to generate income from their ideas. A shift from a dependence on land development and tourism to the improvement of an environment for collaboration, creativity and the support for business success is imperative.
